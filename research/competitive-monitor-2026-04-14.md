## Whitespace Opportunities

### 1. Africa-specific fraud detection — entirely unclaimed
No competitor — including BetConstruct — has a product page, press release, or conference talk addressing mobile money fraud, SIM swap abuse, retail agent collusion, or USSD session fraud. BetConstruct's Umbrella AI detects multi-accounts and game styles; it is not designed around African fraud infrastructure. B2Tech, with an Africa-native positioning and a dedicated Risk Manager, is best placed to own this. Directly maps to current quarter priorities.

### 2. Risk Ops tooling for 1–2 person teams
Africa operators run lean risk teams. BetConstruct, EveryMatrix, and Digitain all build for enterprise risk operations. A risk ops product designed for low-staff operators — simplified alert queues, auto-decisioning defaults, mobile-accessible case management — has no current market owner.

### 3. USSD-native risk controls
Altenar has a USSD betting channel but no evidence of fraud controls specific to USSD sessions (session hijacking, duplicate USSD accounts, stolen MSISDN). If B2Tech builds USSD, layering risk controls specific to the channel would be genuinely novel.

### 4. Operator-configurable risk thresholds — B2B differentiator
BetConstruct's Umbrella AI is a platform-level product — operators configure within constraints the platform sets. In a B2B model, giving operators visible control over their own risk rules and thresholds (with full audit trail) is a buying argument that no competitor currently leads on publicly.

### 5. Progressive KYC for low-identity markets
EveryMatrix's South Africa push targets a relatively high-infrastructure market. Markets like DRC, Cameroon, and Zambia — where SIM-based onboarding, offline retail KYC, and low-end Android liveness are the only viable paths — remain unaddressed by all tracked competitors.
